Makeblock Ultimate 2.0 Kit

The Makeblock Ultimate 2.0 Kit is a comprehensive robot kit for beginners to explore the robotic world or experts to build complex robots. It contains everything you need to construct your robot dreams: metal geared motors, Arduino-compatible controller, programmable RGB LED strip, different kinds of sensors, robotic gripper and other mechanical parts. It is also great for learning Arduino and Scratch programming. You can control your robot with your smart device through a Bluetooth connection.

Features

  • Great for learning robotics, electronics and Arduino programming
  • Aluminum extrusion structure
  • Easy wiring, no soldering required
  • Bluetooth connection lets you control your robot with your smartphone or tablet

Programming and Control

Smart device app Control: Install the Makeblock app to control your creations with your Android or iOS device.

Scratch programming: Makeblock platform supports graphical programming language Scratch, you can simply drag and joint the blocks to run your creations without difficult coding.

Arduino programming

Arduino is an open-source electronics prototyping platform based on flexible, easy to use hardware and software.

Arduino Uno

The Arduino Uno is a widely used open-source microcontroller board designed for beginners and experienced users alike, serving as a foundational platform for electronics and coding projects. It is based on the ATmega328P microcontroller and is known for its robustness, extensive documentation, and large community support.

Key Features and Specifications

The Arduino Uno is equipped with 14 digital input/output pins, six of which can be used for Pulse Width Modulation (PWM) outputs, and 6 analog input pins. It operates at 5V and is recommended to be powered with an input voltage between 7-12V.

  • Microcontroller: ATmega328P
  • Operating Voltage: 5V
  • Input Voltage: 7-12V
  • Digital I/O Pins: 14 (6 with PWM output)
  • Analog Input Pins: 6
  • Flash Memory: 32 KB
  • Clock Speed: 16 MHz

Power and Connectivity

The Arduino Uno can be powered via its USB connection or an external power supply. It supports serial communication, I2C, and SPI protocols.

Programming and Development

Programming the Arduino Uno is done using the Arduino IDE, which supports a C++-like programming language. The Uno is an open-source hardware design.

mBot - Entry Level Robot Kit

The mBot is an entry-level educational robot kit designed to make learning about robotics and programming fun and accessible for beginners. It is developed by Makeblock and is aimed at children to help them develop skills in STEAM.

Core Features and Educational Value

The mBot is designed for easy assembly, often requiring only a screwdriver and taking about 10-15 minutes. It utilizes color-coded RJ25 ports for simplified wiring.

  • Ease of Assembly: Quick construction without soldering
  • Programming: Supports graphical block-based programming (Scratch) and Arduino IDE
  • Sensors: Ultrasonic, line-following, light sensors, RGB LEDs, buzzer
  • Expandability: Compatible with Makeblock ecosystem and LEGO bricks

Software and Programming

mBlock (based on Scratch 3.0), Makeblock App, and mBlock Blockly App are available for programming and control.

Technical Specifications

ATmega328 microcontroller, 3.7V lithium battery or 4 AA batteries, dimensions 190×130×130 mm, weight ~500g, Bluetooth or 2.4G wireless connectivity.

ASHS History

Apayao Science High School Today

Located on top of a mountain viewing the beauty of nature stands the Apayao Science High School, that was created under RA 8896 authored by then Congressman of the Lone District of Apayao, Hon. ELIAS K. BULUT. This converted the High School Department of the Apayao State College to Apayao Science High School located at San Isidro Sur, Luna, Apayao on August 22, 2000.

Tracing its origin, the high school department of the Apayao State College was the former Luna Academy, a private school that became an annex of the Kaling-Apayao Schools of Arts and Trades located at Malama, Conner, Apayao on June 1, 1993. As an annex the high school department maintained the curriculum of the private school and the former school of the Luna Academy, Betty L. Racacho was assigned as the Officer In-Charge for both high school and college department for four school years. From 1997-2002 Betty L. Racacho opted to lead and manage the secondary department only.

After the signing of the RA 8896 in 2000, Hon. Elias K. Bulut, Sr. opened the Apayao Science High School in the next school year 2001-2002. On the first year of operation ASHS operated without any budget in the GAA. Betty L. Racacho was assigned as Teacher In-Charge, with her are the following teachers who taught in the first year of operation of the said school, Ruth A. Tumaneng, Reymarie M. Caban, Edgar S. Valdez, Luningning B. Aribbay, Romy A. Rigates, Antonio Manuel, Sonia A. Belarde, Crispina Pelobello, Cristeta Arimas, Leah Acidera, Dely Aguite, Sylvia F. Baria, Marcel Ubay and Imelda Bautista.

On January 5, 2007, the 1-storey building with three rooms was burned which included the newly purchased laboratory apparatuses, books, set of encyclopedias solicited from Hawaii by the officers of the PTA, printers, sports equipment and personal reference of teachers, thus classes were conducted at the San Isidro Gymnasium for two weeks.

Former Luna Academy

Apayao Science High School

The Deed of Grant of Usufructuary Rights was formally handed on January 13, 2008 at Soriano's Notary Public Office, Abulug, Cagayan. On July 4, 2011, Betty L. Racacho was designated as the Teacher In-Charge.

In 2020, the Schools Division of Apayao through SDS Benedicta B. Gamatero assigned Mr. Danilo L. Cudiamat to head the school. This was the second year of the COVID 19 pandemic. In response, he and the whole teaching staff established online platforms such as the Student Information and Accounting System (SIAS)- LMS together with available online applications in order to ensure learning continuity amidst the challenges posed by the pandemic.
